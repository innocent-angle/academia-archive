Storing Money

 

Piggy Bank (new players only)

 

During your first week, you may have a piggy bank available under your missions to buy with mission credits. You can put an unlimited(?) amount of money in it, but can only withdraw the money once, by breaking it. It then becomes unusable. Consider grabbing it even if you won't use it, since it's cheap and can't be obtained again in the future.

 

Faction Vault (safe but not convenient)

 

You can store money in our faction vault, but we don't necessarily recommend it. It's not meant to be convenient, as you can't withdraw it yourself, it has to be sent to you on request. The preferred way to request money is to ping the @banker role in Discord in the banking channel, but if you can't join Discord just message me and I'll try to get it to you when it's safe.

 

Stock Market (recommended)

 

By buying a stock ticker from the points market, you gain access to the stock market. You can store an unlimited amount of money here, but there is a 0.1% fee for withdrawing. This is the ideal way to store money long term, as the benefit blocks huge chunks of passive income, such as SYM offering ~70% yearly ROI. Stocks also, on average, increase 10% yearly (spread across all stocks, not individually) so having many stock blocks is excellent.

 

Ghost Trades (recommended, has risks)

 

By starting a trade with someone who has been online in the last 6 hours, you can store an unlimited(?) amount of money in the trade for up to 6 hours. Make sure you withdraw the money and properly store it, because this trade will expire after 6 hours and any money inside will go back into your hand. A few people use me, Cute, for ghost trades and you're welcome to as well. Only do this with people you trust, as the other player could cancel the trade early and mug you.

 

Company Vault (recommended, has risks)

 

Company vaults are in my opinion the most convenient way to store money, but obviously you have to be director of a company. Some players buy cheap companies and don't actually run them, just to use this feature. If you don't own the SYS stock, your company can be hacked and lose 1-3% of funds.

 

Private Island Vault (recommended, limit 1b)

 

Pretty simple, if you own a private island with a vault, you can store money in it. Probably the safest way to store money overall, but you can only store up to 1b if you have the largest size vault. Do not enable spouse vault sharing unless you trust your spouse.

 

Cayman Islands (terrible)

 

You can travel to the Cayman Islands and store your money there, but it's generally a terrible idea. People are always camped out there mugging, at some point no matter how careful you are you will slip up and get mugged. It's not great long term either, the interest rate is 0.5% monthly, which is less yearly than the stock market on average.
